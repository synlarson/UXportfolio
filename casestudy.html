The Local Agenda

My Role: UX/UI Designer
Tools Used: Figma, Miro, Google Drive

The Problem:

People are ready to travel again after many months of COVID-19 related restrictions. Young people feel open to solo travel because of the flexibility, but are worried about missing out on social experinces and local insights that come from traveling with or to visit friends.

The Solution:

The Local Agenda is a travel app that will help solo travelers feel less intimated in a new place; by connecting them with locals and fellow travelers to discover unique experiences

The Impact:

Created an app that would allow solo travelers to connect with others and feel safe in the process.

For this project we were tasked with creating the onboarding process for an app to help travelers. We began by conducting 5 one on one interviews to gather insights about there general attitudes and habits around travel.

The insights from our user interviews told us young adults in their 20's want to experiance traveling solo
From here we reconfigured our questions and conducted 5 more user interviews focusing on solo travel.

    53% of our respondents said they have solo traveled.

    25% of our resdpondents said they want to travel alone.

Ideation and Definition

User Persona: Meet Hannah Benham.

Hannah wants to experiance traveling solo but is intimidated by the thought of being alone in a new place.

Pain Points:

Get’s frustrated when trying to accommodate for other peoples interests
Doesn’t feel safe traveling alone
Feels lonely when traveling solo
Finds making friends on other services difficult.

Problem Statement

Travelers like Hannah need a way to help them safe and confident while traveling alone to gain the most out of their experiance.

Testing and Iterations

The next step was to test our onboarding prototype with users here is what we found:

Users perfered drop down menus to having to enter a destination

Buttons for going back to the previous page need to be more prominent

Users in their 20’s liked the onboarding resembulence to a dating app as they are already comfortable with the process.

Final Product and Conclusion

Conclusion:

This being my first UX design project I learned a lot. The main takeways is that our team learned how to work well together to produce the best solution for our problem.

Next Steps:

If I were to continue to build out the rest of the app I would do more user research about the saftey of solo travel and build out a feature to help travelers feel safe while traveling alone.
